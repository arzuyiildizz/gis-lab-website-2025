Study Area

Air pollution, caused by harmful gases and particulates in the atmosphere, poses serious risks to both human health and the environment. Key sources include industrial activities, vehicle emissions, fossil fuel combustion, and natural phenomena such as wildfires and volcanic eruptions. These pollutants not only accelerate climate change but also lead to respiratory problems and environmental degradation.

This project explores the spatial and temporal patterns of major air pollutants—such as NO₂, PM₂.₅, and PM₁₀—by utilizing open-access geospatial datasets (e.g., from Copernicus). Through standard GIS techniques including data preprocessing, classification, and advanced visualization, we aim to analyze pollution trends in relation to land cover types and identify exceedances above recommended thresholds.

The findings are presented via an interactive WebGIS platform, offering users an intuitive way to explore pollution levels across space and time. This webpage serves as an introduction to the laboratory work, highlighting the methodology, data sources, and key outcomes of the study.
